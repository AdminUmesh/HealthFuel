# Vitamin B12 (Cobalamin) – Complete Notes

## 1. What is Vitamin B12?
Vitamin B12 (Cobalamin) is a **water‑soluble vitamin** essential for:
- Red blood cell formation  
- Brain and nervous system health  
- DNA synthesis  
- Energy metabolism  

---

## 2. Daily Requirement (RDA)

**Adult men & women (18–50 years):**
- **2.4 micrograms (µg) per day**

> Body weight, height, or gender does NOT significantly change B12 requirement.

---

## 3. Natural Sources of Vitamin B12

Vitamin B12 is found **only in animal-based foods**.

### Best Sources
- Fish (salmon, tuna, sardine)
- Chicken, mutton
- Eggs
- Milk, curd, paneer, cheese

### Important Note
- **Vegetarian & vegan diets → high risk of deficiency**
- Plant foods do NOT provide reliable B12

---

## 4. Causes of Vitamin B12 Deficiency
- Vegetarian/vegan diet
- Poor absorption (gastritis, low stomach acid)
- Long-term use of antacids or metformin
- Alcohol abuse
- Aging

---

## 5. Symptoms of Vitamin B12 Deficiency

### Early Symptoms
- Fatigue, weakness
- Poor concentration
- Mood changes (anxiety, depression)

### Nervous System Symptoms
- Tingling or numbness in hands & feet
- Burning sensation in feet
- Memory problems
- Brain fog

### Blood-related
- Megaloblastic anemia
- Pale or yellowish skin
- Shortness of breath

> ⚠️ Long-term deficiency may cause **permanent nerve damage**

---

## 6. Diagnosis

### Blood Test
- **Serum Vitamin B12**

### Reference Levels
- **< 200 pg/mL** → Deficient  
- **200–300 pg/mL** → Borderline  
- **300–900 pg/mL** → Normal  

---

## 7. Supplementation (Methylcobalamin)

### Common Doses
- 500 mcg
- **1000 mcg (1 mg)** – commonly prescribed

### How to Take
**Deficiency treatment**
- 1000 mcg daily for 4–8 weeks
- Then once weekly or monthly

**Maintenance (vegetarian)**
- 1000 mcg once weekly  
OR  
- 500 mcg 2–3 times per week

> Vitamin B12 is very safe. Excess is excreted in urine.

---

## 8. Injection vs Tablet

| Tablets | Injections |
|------|-----------|
| Mild/moderate deficiency | Severe deficiency |
| Easy & painless | Faster correction |
| Long-term use | Short-term use |

---

## 9. Storage of Vitamin B12 in Body
- Stored mainly in the **liver**
- Storage lasts **2–5 years**
- Deficiency develops slowly

---

## 10. Recovery Timeline

| Improvement Area | Time |
|----------------|------|
| Energy | 1–2 weeks |
| Mood & focus | 2–4 weeks |
| Anemia | 4–8 weeks |
| Nerve symptoms | 1–3 months |

---

## 11. Key Takeaways
- Daily need: **2.4 µg**
- Vegetarians should supplement
- Deficiency affects nerves & blood
- Methylcobalamin is safe & effective
- Early treatment prevents permanent damage
