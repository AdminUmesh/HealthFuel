
# Magnesium – Complete Notes

## 1. What is Magnesium?
Magnesium is an **essential mineral** involved in more than **300 enzyme reactions** in the body. It plays a key role in:
- Muscle function and relaxation
- Nerve signaling
- Energy production (ATP)
- Protein synthesis
- Blood sugar regulation
- Blood pressure control
- Bone health
- Sleep quality

The body cannot make magnesium, so it must be obtained from food or supplements.

---

## 2. Common Forms of Magnesium

| Form | Best For | Absorption |
|------|----------|------------|
| **Magnesium Glycinate** | Sleep, anxiety, muscle relaxation | High |
| **Magnesium Citrate** | Constipation, general use | High |
| **Magnesium Malate** | Energy, fatigue, fibromyalgia | High |
| **Magnesium Threonate** | Brain, focus, memory | High (crosses blood-brain barrier) |
| **Magnesium Oxide** | Constipation (laxative) | Low |
| **Magnesium Chloride** | Topical use, general | Moderate–High |
| **Magnesium Sulfate** | Epsom salt baths, IV use | Low (oral) |

> **Glycinate** and **Citrate** are the most popular for daily supplementation.

---

## 3. Daily Requirement (RDA)

| Category | Magnesium |
|----------|-----------|
| Men (19–30) | **400 mg/day** |
| Men (31+) | **420 mg/day** |
| Women (19–30) | **310 mg/day** |
| Women (31+) | **320 mg/day** |
| Pregnancy | **350–360 mg/day** |
| Breastfeeding | **310–320 mg/day** |
| Upper limit (from supplements) | **350 mg/day** |

> Food sources do not count toward the upper limit — only supplements.

- Almonds, cashews, peanuts
- Spinach, Swiss chard
- Black beans, kidney beans, chickpeas
- Whole grains (oats, brown rice, quinoa)
- Dark chocolate (70%+)
- Avocado
- Banana
- Tofu

### Non-Veg
- Salmon
- Mackerel
- Chicken breast
- Eggs (small amount)

## 5. How Magnesium Helps the Body

### Muscle & Recovery
- Reduces muscle cramps
- Helps post-workout recovery
- Supports muscle relaxation

### Sleep & Stress
- Calms the nervous system
- Improves sleep quality
- Reduces anxiety
- Supports relaxation

### Heart & Blood
- Maintains normal heart rhythm
- Helps regulate blood pressure
- Supports healthy blood sugar levels

### Bones
- Works with calcium and vitamin D
- Required for bone density

### Energy
- Required for ATP production
- Reduces fatigue

---

## 6. Causes of Magnesium Deficiency
- Low intake of green vegetables, nuts, seeds
- High intake of processed food
- Heavy alcohol use
- Chronic stress
- Excessive coffee or soda
- Diabetes
- GI disorders (Crohn's, celiac)
- Certain medications (PPIs, diuretics)
- Heavy sweating / endurance training

---

## 7. Symptoms of Deficiency

### Mild
- Muscle cramps or twitches
- Fatigue
- Poor sleep
- Headaches
- Irritability

### Moderate–Severe
- Numbness or tingling
- Abnormal heart rhythm
- Anxiety / restlessness
- Low calcium or potassium levels
- Seizures (rare)

---

## 8. Who May Need Extra Magnesium?
- Gym-goers / athletes (lost in sweat)
- Poor sleepers
- High stress lifestyle
- People with cramps or restless legs
- Diabetics
- Heavy coffee/alcohol drinkers
- Vegetarians with low nut/seed intake
- Older adults

---

## 9. Suggested Use (General)
- **Dose:** 200–400 mg elemental magnesium per day
- **Best time:** Evening or 30–60 minutes before bed (especially Glycinate)
- **With or without food:** With food reduces stomach upset
- **Split doses** (morning + night) improve absorption if dose > 300 mg

---

## 10. Avoid Taking Same Time With
- Calcium supplements (compete for absorption)
- Iron tablets
- Zinc (high dose)
- Antibiotics (tetracyclines, quinolones)
- Thyroid medication

> Keep a **2–3 hour gap**.

---

## 11. Side Effects (if too much)
- Loose motion / diarrhea (most common, especially Citrate/Oxide)
- Nausea
- Stomach cramps
- Low blood pressure (very high doses)

> If you get loose motions, lower the dose or switch to **Glycinate**.

---

## 12. Toxicity (Rare)
Usually only from very high supplement doses or kidney disease:
- Severe diarrhea
- Slow heart rate
- Confusion
- Muscle weakness
- Kidney problems

---

## 13. Magnesium + Sleep
- **Glycinate** is most effective for sleep
- Take **30–60 minutes before bed**
- Combine with good sleep hygiene
- Often pairs well with vitamin D3 and zinc

---

## 14. Magnesium + Workout
- Helps reduce post-workout cramps
- Supports recovery and ATP regeneration
- Useful for endurance athletes who sweat heavily
- Can be taken **post-workout or at night**

---

## 15. Best Plan
- Eat magnesium-rich foods daily (seeds, nuts, greens)
- Limit excessive coffee and alcohol
- Manage stress and sleep
- Use supplement only if diet is low or symptoms exist
- Choose the **right form** for your goal

---

## 16. Key Takeaways
- Magnesium supports muscle, nerve, sleep, heart, and energy
- **Glycinate** = best for sleep & relaxation
- **Citrate** = best for general use & constipation
- 200–400 mg/day is a safe range
- Take in evening, away from calcium and iron
- Loose motion = signal to reduce dose
